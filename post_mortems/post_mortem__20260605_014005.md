# INCIDENT TITLE:
Payment Service Connection Pool Exhaustion Incident

# SEVERITY:
Critical

# DATE & DURATION:

# EXECUTIVE SUMMARY:
A critical incident occurred in our payment service, causing failed orders, transaction rollbacks, and a loss of database connection. The issue was triggered by a sudden increase in traffic, exceeding the connection pool capacity. Our team responded promptly, and the issue was resolved by increasing the connection pool size and implementing connection pooling. We are taking steps to prevent similar incidents in the future, including scaling out our database and implementing circuit breakers.

# TECHNICAL SUMMARY:
The payment service experienced a connection pool exhaustion, leading to a series of downstream errors, including failed transactions, timeouts, and ultimately a loss of database connection. The root cause of the incident was a sudden and significant increase in traffic, exceeding the connection pool capacity.

# IMPACT ANALYSIS:
The incident resulted in failed orders, transaction rollbacks, and a loss of database connection, causing a significant impact on the system's functionality. The estimated revenue loss during the incident is approximately $10,000.

# ROOT CAUSE:
The primary root cause of the incident is a sudden and significant increase in traffic, exceeding the connection pool capacity. Contributing factors include inadequate connection pool sizing, insufficient monitoring and alerting, tight coupling between services, and a lack of circuit breakers or bulkheads.

# WHAT WENT WELL:
* The system's health checks and monitoring detected the issue, although not soon enough to prevent the failure.
* The api-gateway and load-balancer continued to operate, albeit with increased latency and errors.
* The order-service initiated compensation workflows, which suggests that it had some level of fault tolerance and error handling in place.

# WHAT WENT WRONG:
* The payment service's connection pool was not sized to handle the maximum expected load, leading to exhaustion under peak conditions.
* The system lacked adequate monitoring and alerting to detect the increasing load and connection pool utilization before it became critical.
* The tight coupling between services meant that failures in the payment service had a direct and significant impact on the order service.

# LESSONS LEARNED:
* The importance of adequate connection pool sizing and monitoring to prevent exhaustion under peak conditions.
* The need for sufficient monitoring and alerting to detect increasing load and connection pool utilization before it becomes critical.
* The benefits of implementing circuit breakers or bulkheads to prevent cascading failures in downstream services.
* The value of regular review and testing of connection pool configuration and database connection metrics to ensure the system remains healthy and performant.
